Brief

After making the Japanese planing board, I found more woodworking videos on YouTube. This introduced me to more woodworking principles and how I could learn them. One such video was yet another documentary about the same wood joinery. A box is shown in it, containing intricate models of wood joints;

Determined to improve my craft, this was the perfect way to learn wood joinery and to refine my skill. The box would act as a reference when jointing in the future. On the box I observed a full blind, double-bevelled dovetail joint. Which, seems to be the ultimate joint from an aesthetic and practical standpoint.

Concept

As before, very little of my own designing was required as the design is shown in the documentary. Nevertheless, I would still need to become acquainted with the traditional Japanese wood joints;

Much of the contained joints were not shown in the video. So the layout and details of the joint models will be designed.

Design

To reduce size, the several joints will be used on the one piece of wood, i.e. two end-to-end joints on one block.

As seen in the concept section, the lid will have wide mortise and tenon joints, the bottom will be a tongue and groove joint and the corners will be curved box/dovetail joints.

There will be 20 main pieces in the box with the following joints per piece;

  • (2) Shouldered M + T, kawai tsugite, lap joint
  • (2) Pole Tenon, double dovetail lap joint, bow-tie
  • (1) Tongue + groove, ninety degree tongue + groove, triple M + T
  • (1) Tongue + groove, ninety degree tongue + groove, triple M + T, Shouldered M + T
  • (2) Half blind dovetail, full blind dovetail
  • (2) Sliding dovetail with key, double sliding dovetail
  • (2) Goose-neck joint, stud tenon scarf
  • (2) Scarf with keys, through mortise and tenon with key
  • (2) Full blind double M + T, three-way M + T mitre
  • (1) Three-way M + T mitre, double pointed M + T
  • (3) Three-way open M + T, chidori joint, sliding dovetail, M + T, sliding dovetail

The curved box joint will be made by making a traditional blind box mitre joint, then adding a strip of wood to the inside to create the inside bevel.

Design Criteria;

  • Box fits models with ample space
  • Box is aesthetically pleasing
  • Box is both strong and easy to disassemble
  • Joint models are made to a high standard, a good reference for joint quality
  • Joint models are slightly loose so they can be constantly assembled and disassembled
  • All components are hand-made and do not require adhesives
  • A full box is light and portable

Designing each of the twenty pieces down to the nearest millimetre would be unrealistic, as the available wood stock may not suit many of the required piece sizes. Therefore, the subsequent drawings of the wood pieces are more for illustrative purposes rather than a direct reference.

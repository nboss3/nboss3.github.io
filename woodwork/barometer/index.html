Brief

Clocks seem to be a very common woodworking project. It was researching clocks that I stumbled across barometers. I found many listings on eBay for 3-in-1 weather stations, that have a barometer, thermometer and hygrometers. Instead having yet another clock project I bought one of the weather stations and used that instead.

Although these barometers can be wall mounted, I wanted to make a decorative casing for it to be seated in. The casing was to be wall mounted and complement the barometer.

Concept

Concept guidelines;

  • The case design needs aesthetics as a priority
  • The case must be able to be wall mounted

Research was conducted through simple internet search results. Clock and barometer housings come in many different styles and constructions so concepts to analyse were chosen on aesthetic merit and the ability to wall mount them.

Most barometer housings are teardrop-shaped like those in Concept 1 & 2, although they are usually more flat and lack carvings. I would like my casing to have ornamentation so Concept 1 & 2 are an excellent guide.

The remaining figures are that of cuckoo clocks and regular clock. These designs can be adapted as they are similar to the styles in Concept 1 & 2. The style of pillars on the sides with finials especially appeals to me. The floral patterns in Concept 4 & 5 are also something I would like to try and replicate.

The design features of floral patterns, spindles, finials, carvings and a house-like structure will be used. The construction will be based on Concept 5, with the gabled roof, mainly box-like construction with overhanging pillars. The style and proportions will need to be significantly altered to match the barometer.

Design

From analysing existing products, the following design features were chosen;

  • A routed socket for the barometer to sit in
  • Finials on the top and bottom of the casing
  • Two pillars running up either side of the barometer casing
  • Floral carvings/embellishments to complement the casings structure
  • A gabled roof on the casing
  • A secondary element to replace the thermometer / cuckoo bird as seen in concepts

The design criteria for the casing were;

  • The assembly must be aesthetically pleasing
  • Casing should fit the barometer properly and be strong enough to allow the barometer to be tapped
  • It should be strong enough so that carvings and ornaments can be touched without breakage
  • The barometer should not be obscured, difficult to operate nor read
  • The casing should be light enough to be wall-mounted
  • It should be small enough to be portable and not take up significant wall space
